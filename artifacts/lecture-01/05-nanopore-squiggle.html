Nanopore Squiggle Decoder

DNA threads through a ~1-nm pore; the bases currently inside partially block an ionic current, producing a stepped time series — the squiggle. A basecaller turns that signal back into a sequence. Here it's just a nearest-neighbour lookup over k-mers — real basecallers use deep learning for a reason.
