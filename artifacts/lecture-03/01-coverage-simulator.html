At α = 0 sampling is uniform and the histogram hugs the Poisson(λ) curve. As α climbs, reads concentrate around preferred regions; the mean stays put but zero-coverage positions explode and the distribution goes bimodal. That is why "30× average" is never a coverage guarantee — and why the Poisson assumption under-predicts real-world dropouts.
