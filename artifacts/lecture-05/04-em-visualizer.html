EM Iteration Visualizer

Step through Expectation-Maximization on a small RNA-seq quantification problem. Three transcripts with known lengths share a pool of reads; some reads are uniquely mappable, others are multi-mapped. Watch the fractional assignments and abundance estimates converge — or fail to, when the compatibility classes leave EM degenerate.

Scenario

Each segment between | is one read. List the transcripts it is compatible with, comma-separated: T1,T2 means the read maps to both T1 and T2. Transcript lengths are fixed at (500, 1500, 2000) across all scenarios.

Iteration control

Each step runs one full EM iteration: the E-step splits every read’s vote across its compatibility class in proportion to θt / ℓt, then the M-step normalises the column sums into the next θ. Convergence is declared when max |Δθ| < 10−6; the safety cap is 200 iterations.

Log-likelihood trajectory

Monotone non-decreasing — a defining property of EM. If the curve flattens early, EM has found a stationary point; if it keeps drifting by tiny amounts, the likelihood surface has a ridge (look at the fully-ambiguous preset).
