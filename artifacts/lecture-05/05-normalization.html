Normalisation Calculator

Four ways to make raw read counts comparable across genes and samples. Edit the matrix below and watch CPM, FPKM, TPM, and DESeq2 size-factor-adjusted counts update in lockstep. The aha: the four units can rank the same genes differently. Raw abundance wins one ordering; per-base density (length correction) wins another.

Normalised outputs

CPM · counts per million

CPMs,g = cs,g / (Ds / 106)

FPKM · per kb per million

FPKMs,g = cs,g / ((Ds/106) · (ℓg/1000))

TPM · transcripts per million

TPMs,g = (cs,g/ℓg) / Σ(cs,·/ℓ·) · 106

Size-factor-adjusted counts

adjs,g = cs,g / sfs  (median-of-ratios, DESeq2)

Click any sample column header to sort by that unit. CPM and size-factor counts only correct for sequencing depth; FPKM and TPM also correct for gene length. TPM sums to exactly 106 per sample — a fixed-total unit, which makes it the only length-aware metric that is comparable across samples without further caveats.

Size factors (median-of-ratios)

For each gene, take the geometric mean of counts across samples. Then for each sample, the size factor is the median of count / geo-mean ratios across genes. Most genes have stable expression, so the median is robust — it resists outliers from truly differentially-expressed genes and gives a depth estimate that's not pulled by a handful of highly-expressed hits.
