Poisson vs Negative Binomial

Count data from RNA-seq has more spread than Poisson predicts. Slide the dispersion α from zero and watch the negative binomial widen while Poisson stays put — then check the real mean-variance scatter underneath to see why every modern DE tool uses NB.

Histograms share the x-axis scale so the difference in spread is visual at a glance. At α = 0.2 and μ = 50 the NB variance is 50 + 0.2·50² = 550 — eleven times the Poisson variance. Push α toward 1 and the NB distribution starts showing a long right tail, exactly what a highly variable gene looks like across samples.
