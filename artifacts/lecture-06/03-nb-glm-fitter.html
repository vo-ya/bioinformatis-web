Negative Binomial GLM Fitter

Fit a two-group NB GLM to a 6-sample toy dataset: log(μi) = log(si) + β0 + β1·xi, where xi=1 for treated. IRLS converges the point estimate; the Fisher information gives standard errors; Wald and a profile-at-β1=0 LRT decide significance. Change counts, size factors, or the fixed dispersion α and watch β̂1, SE, and both p-values recompute.

Dispersion & refit

Variance: Var(y) = μ + α·μ². Poisson is α = 0 (left edge). Cranking α up widens both SEs without moving the point estimate much — watch the p-values slide.

At n = 6 these two tests are asymptotically equivalent but differ in finite samples — they should agree to within roughly 20% on the p-value at this size. If you see a huge gap, something degenerate is happening (e.g. zero counts in one arm).
