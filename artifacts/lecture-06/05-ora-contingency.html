ORA Contingency Explorer

Over-representation analysis (ORA) tests whether a gene set — e.g. G2/M checkpoint — is enriched among your differentially expressed genes. The 2×2 contingency table feeds Fisher's exact test (hypergeometric tail) and a chi-square approximation. Slide the four knobs and watch the p-value, enrichment ratio, and tail probability update live. Compare the default with the small overlap preset to see how a handful of "enriched" genes can still be statistically invisible.

·
Adjust the sliders to compute enrichment.
Fisher's exact test is the gold standard for 2×2 count data. The chi-square approximation agrees when all expected counts ≥ 5.

Default preset lands near p ≈ 1e-8, 13× enrichment — a textbook significant hit. Drop Overlap to 5: expected is ~3, observed is 5, ratio is 1.6× — Fisher's p climbs above 0.2. Enrichment is relative to expected, and small absolute numbers are surprisingly insignificant.
