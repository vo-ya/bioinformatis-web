UMI Deduplication Demo

Each mRNA molecule is tagged with a (cell barcode, UMI) pair before PCR amplifies it thousands of times. When we collapse reads that share the same (CB, UMI) back to a single molecule, the PCR factor drops out — the final count is invariant to amplification. Turn the PCR cycles slider up: total reads explode, but the recovered molecule count does not budge.

Pipeline

STEP 1 Original mRNAs in each cell

The cell contents we want to quantify. Each box is one mRNA molecule; colours uniquely identify a molecule across all downstream steps.

STEP 2 Tagged with cell barcode + UMI

10x chemistry attaches a fixed cell barcode (CB) per droplet and a random UMI per mRNA capture. The (CB, UMI) pair is the unique molecule fingerprint we will dedup on later.

STEP 3 PCR amplification

Each tagged molecule is copied 2cycles times. The PCR copies all share their parent's (CB, UMI) tag — that is what makes dedup possible later.

STEP 4 Sequencing (random sample of reads)

The sequencer only reads a fraction of the amplified library. Each chip below is one sequenced read, coloured by its source molecule.

STEP 5 Deduplication — collapse by (CB, UMI)

Group reads by their (CB, UMI) pair; keep one per group. The recovered molecule count is back to the original — the PCR factor vanishes.
