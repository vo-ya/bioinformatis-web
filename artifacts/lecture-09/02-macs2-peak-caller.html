MACS2 Peak Caller

Slide a 200 bp window across simulated ChIP-seq coverage. At each position, compute a local Poisson rate from nested windows (1 kb, 5 kb, up to your chosen size) and take the maximum — that is MACS2's CFAR trick. A peak is only called when the window count is improbably high against its own neighbourhood, so a local bump in baseline — a repeat, a copy-number amplicon, an ATAC-seq open chromatin region — cannot fake a peak. Toggle local λ off with the noisy region on and watch the false positives bloom.

Try the canonical demo: turn the noisy region on, keep local λ on — clean precision. Then switch local λ off. The genome-wide λ underestimates the noise inside the amber stripe, and a crop of red false positives sprouts there. The compare cards always show both regimes side by side so the effect is visible without toggling.
