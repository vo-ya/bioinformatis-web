Three-Letter Alignment Demo

A bisulfite-converted read won't align cleanly to the four-letter reference: every unmethylated C has become a T, so naive Smith-Waterman sees mismatches everywhere. The fix is to convert both sides, reference and read, C-to-T in silico and align in three-letter space. After the read is placed we decode methylation by looking back at each CpG's original read base: C stayed C (methylated), C turned into T (unmethylated).
