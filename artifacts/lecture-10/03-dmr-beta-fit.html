DMR Beta Fit Explorer

A single CpG, two groups, Beta-Binomial conjugate updating. Simulate reads per replicate, pool m/n per group, then fit Beta(α + Σm, β + Σ(n−m)) to each arm. The plot overlays the two posterior densities, shades the 95% credible intervals, and reports P(μ_A > μ_B | data) from 20,000 paired Monte-Carlo draws. Crank the reads down: the curves fatten, the intervals overlap, and posterior certainty evaporates even though the true means haven't moved.

Conjugate update: prior Beta(α, β) + observed Σm methylated out of Σn reads gives posterior Beta(α + Σm, β + Σ(n−m)). The 95% CI is the central-mass quantile interval. P(A>B) is the Monte-Carlo fraction of paired draws where the A sample exceeds the B sample.
