ICE Normalisation Demo

Hi-C contact matrices carry per-bin biases — differences in mappability, restriction-site density, GC content. Left untreated, biased bins shout as bright horizontal and vertical bands. Iterative Correction and Eigenvector decomposition (ICE) assumes every bin should have equal visibility: normalise row sums, then column sums, repeat. Banding fades; biology remains.

Step through a few iterations and watch the bright bands in the left heatmap fade. Each ICE pass divides every row by its sum (relative to the mean row sum) and every column by its sum — the operation is symmetric, so the matrix stays symmetric. CV drops quickly for the first few passes, then asymptotes. Pushing bias strength to 5× makes the initial banding far louder, but ICE still converges; it just takes more iterations.
