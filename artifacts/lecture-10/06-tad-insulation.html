TAD Insulation Score Calculator

A Hi-C contact matrix is a 2D object, but a topological domain boundary is a 1D event — a position along the diagonal where contacts suddenly stop crossing. The insulation score collapses the 2D matrix to a 1D track: slide a square window down the diagonal, measure the mean contact intensity crossing the corner, and take log2 of that versus the local expectation. TAD boundaries fall out as sharp local minima. Default settings reproduce the canonical domain-caller behaviour; shrink the window to 3 bins and watch the track fragment into spurious minima.
