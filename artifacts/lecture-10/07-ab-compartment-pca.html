A/B Compartment Eigenvector

A simulated observed-over-expected (O/E) Hi-C matrix with alternating A and B compartments produces a checkerboard pattern. Take its correlation matrix. Run power iteration. The first principal component's sign flips where the compartment flips: PC1 is the A/B call. This is the cleanest demonstration of genomics as linear algebra.

log-O/E contacts. Bins in the same compartment are enriched (red); different compartments are depleted (blue). This is the checkerboard signal PC1 will find.

(b) Correlation matrix · sharper checkerboard

Pearson correlation between bin rows. Two bins in the same compartment have similar contact profiles, so their rows correlate strongly. The checkerboard is now saturated at +1 / -1.
