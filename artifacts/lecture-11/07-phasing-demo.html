Haplotype Phasing Demo

Plant a diploid region with heterozygous SNPs, simulate short (150 bp) and long (15–25 kb) reads over it, and watch each technology attempt to phase the variants. Short reads connect hets only when a single fragment spans both — phase blocks stay small and break at gaps. Long reads span most of the region in one go and resolve phase directly, producing one chromosome-scale block.

Pairwise accuracy = fraction of adjacent het-SNP pairs whose inferred phase matches the planted truth. A switch at a block boundary is essentially a coin-flip, so each break costs ~½ of one pair. Long reads almost never need to break a block; short reads break whenever no fragment bridges two neighbouring hets.
