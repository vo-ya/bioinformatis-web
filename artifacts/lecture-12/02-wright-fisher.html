Wright-Fisher Drift + Selection Simulator

Many replicate populations, identical parameters, different random fates. Each generation, every allele is resampled from the expected post-selection frequency via Binomial(2N, p′). Watch individual trajectories scatter around the mean sweep curve — then change N and s independently. The regime label tracks 2Ns, not either parameter alone: double N, halve s, and the outcome is indistinguishable.

Default (N = 1000, s = +0.01, p₀ = 0.05) gives 2Ns = 20: most replicates fix, median fixation near a few hundred generations. Drop N to 50 with s = +0.01 → 2Ns = 1 and drift wins: P(fix) collapses toward the neutral expectation of p₀. Push s to +0.2 at N = 50 → 2Ns = 20 again, and the sweep returns. The product 2Ns is the knob; N and s individually are not.
