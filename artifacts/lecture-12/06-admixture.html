ADMIXTURE Component Explorer

Simulate a cohort drawn from Ktrue ancestral populations (with some admixed individuals) and fit an ADMIXTURE-style mixture model for a chosen Kfit. With the right K, recovered components track the truth at > 0.9 correlation; push K too high and the model obligingly splits a real cluster in two. Five-fold cross-validation error across Kfit ∈ {2..7} reveals the model-selection minimum — which coincides with the true K when the signal is clean, not because ADMIXTURE recovers biology but because the mixture likelihood stops improving.

K-sweep · 5-fold CV error

CV error is the held-out negative log-likelihood per allele call: mask 20% of genotypes, refit, and score the masked cells' Bernoulli likelihood under the fitted Q, P. Curves bottom out near Ktrue; beyond it, the model is overparameterised and held-out likelihood flattens or rebounds even as in-sample log-likelihood keeps rising.

ADMIXTURE itself uses block-coordinate ascent on a multinomial likelihood; here we use the same multiplicative updates (Q-step then P-step, holding the other fixed). For tractability we run ~60 EM iterations and compute CV with 6 inner EM iterations per fold. This is a teaching-scale implementation — production ADMIXTURE handles hundreds of thousands of SNPs, but the qualitative behaviour around K selection is identical.
