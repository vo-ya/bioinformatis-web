Selection Scan — iHS and Tajima’s D

Simulate a genomic region with an optional planted selective sweep. Compute Tajima’s D and a simplified iHS in non-overlapping 10 kb windows, then plot both as Manhattan-style scans. Flag windows above the significance threshold and compare to the known sweep location. The aha: a single extreme window is not a claim about selection — it is a candidate to validate against a properly calibrated null, ideally with multiple statistics.
