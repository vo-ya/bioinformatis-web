Anatomy of a FASTQ record

Four lines per read. The sequence and quality strings must have equal length.

Every FASTQ record is four lines. Lines 2 and 4 must share the same length — one quality character per base. The Phred score Q encodes the probability that the base call is wrong; in the modern Phred+33 encoding (used by all current Illumina, PacBio, and ONT output), the ASCII value of the quality character minus 33 gives Q. Here, the character I at position 14 encodes Q = 40, meaning a 1-in-10,000 chance the base call is wrong.
