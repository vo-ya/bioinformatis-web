Bioinformatics for Engineers

How biology became a data problem.

A one-semester tour of the instrumentation, signal processing, and algorithms that turned biology into an engineering discipline. From the physical structure of the genome to the algorithms that read it — sequencers as microscopes, basecallers as signal processors, the central dogma as a pipeline. Everything framed for engineers: where the signal is, where the noise is, and how the instrument was designed.

Lectures

  1. 01

    Foundations — From Cells to Sequences to FASTQ

    DNA and the central dogma, sequencing history, NGS, long-read platforms, FASTQ.

    ≈ 3h 35min · 12 figures · 6 interactive tools
  2. 02

    Read Alignment — From Brute Force to FM Index and Back

    Indexing the reference (suffix arrays, hash k-mer indices, BWT/FM index), Smith-Waterman DP, CIGAR, and seed-and-extend.

    ≈ 3h 50min · 10 figures · 6 interactive tools
  3. 03

    DNA Sequence Assembly — From Reads to Reconstructed Genomes

    De novo vs resequencing, coverage and k-mer error correction, de Bruijn and overlap graphs, Hierholzer's algorithm, graph cleanup, scaffolding, N50 and assembly metrics.

    ≈ 3h 25min · 11 figures · 6 interactive tools
  4. 04

    Variant Calling — From Aligned Reads to Called Differences

    Variant taxonomy, pileup format, Bayesian genotype likelihoods, the pre-calling pipeline (duplicates, INDEL realignment, BQSR), somatic vs germline callers, structural variants, VCF format, and variant annotation.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 11 figures · 6 interactive tools
  5. 05

    Bulk RNA-seq — From Reads to Transcript Abundances

    Transcription and splicing, splice-aware alignment (STAR, HISAT2), pseudoalignment and EM-based quantification (Kallisto, Salmon), CPM/TPM/FPKM normalisation, and the negative binomial model for count data.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 11 figures · 6 interactive tools
  6. 06

    Differential Expression and Count Statistics

    The negative-binomial GLM, empirical-Bayes dispersion shrinkage (DESeq2 / edgeR), Wald vs LRT tests, Benjamini–Hochberg FDR control at 20k-gene scale, and gene-set enrichment with ORA and GSEA.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 11 figures · 6 interactive tools
  7. 07

    Single-Cell RNA-seq Fundamentals

    Droplet chemistry, cell barcodes and UMIs, empty-droplet detection and the knee plot, per-cell QC, normalisation on overdispersed counts, PCA → UMAP, graph-based clustering, and marker-gene annotation.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 11 figures · 6 interactive tools
  8. 08

    Advanced Single-Cell: Trajectories, Integration, Multi-Modal

    Pseudotime from snapshots, RNA velocity as a state-space estimator on spliced/unspliced ratios, Harmony and scVI batch integration, CITE-seq and scATAC joint embeddings, Visium/MERFISH/Xenium spatial transcriptomics with mixed-pixel deconvolution, and ligand-receptor communication inference.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 10 figures · 6 interactive tools
  9. 09

    ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, and Peak Calling

    Detection theory on the genome — ChIP and ATAC chemistry, MACS2 peak calling as CFAR detection, negative-binomial differential binding, PWM scanning as matched filtering, ATAC footprinting as √N averaging, and Enformer-era sequence-to-regulation prediction.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  10. 10

    Methylation, Hi-C, and 3D Genome Organisation

    Bisulfite sequencing as a channel-flip decoding problem, Beta-Binomial differential methylation, Hi-C contact matrices end-to-end, and a linear-algebra view where A/B compartments are PCA, TADs are change-point detection, and 3D reconstruction is MDS on a distance matrix.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 8 interactive tools
  11. 11

    Long Reads and the Pangenome

    PacBio HiFi and Oxford Nanopore in 2024, SVs / tandem repeats / phasing unlocked, the pangenome shift, graph-based alignment as Viterbi on a DAG, T2T-CHM13 and HPRC v1 as state of the art, and phasing as source separation.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 8 interactive tools
  12. 12

    Population Genetics Fundamentals

    Allele frequencies and Hardy-Weinberg, Wright-Fisher drift + selection as a Markov chain, linkage disequilibrium as genome-scale autocorrelation, the coalescent as backward-in-time stochastic process, demographic inference with PSMC and ADMIXTURE, and reading the signal of selection from mutation patterns.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  13. 13

    GWAS and Statistical Genetics

    Per-SNP regression and the Wald test as a matched filter, genome-wide significance via Bonferroni on effective tests, Manhattan and QQ plots, ancestry confounding and PCA correction as noise whitening, mixed models on structured covariance, polygenic risk scores and portability, fine-mapping as a sparse inverse problem, colocalization with eQTLs, and rare-variant aggregation with SKAT.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  14. 14

    Data Engineering, File Formats, and Reproducibility

    The engineering layer under every algorithm in this course: FASTQ/BAM/CRAM/VCF/BCF/HDF5/Zarr/Parquet as partial-access problems, public repositories split open vs controlled, GRCh38 reference management, containers and conda, workflow languages as dataflow runtimes, GIAB benchmarking via hap.py, and the field's open-source / preprint-first / institution-anchored culture.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  15. 15

    Protein Structure Prediction in the AlphaFold Era

    Fifty years from Anfinsen's refolding experiment to a 90 GDT_TS at CASP14: MSA coevolution as inverse-covariance estimation, the Evoformer's axial attention on a learned pair representation, SE(3)-equivariant Invariant Point Attention, AlphaFold3 diffusion + ligands, ESMFold's single-sequence shortcut, ProteinMPNN inverse folding, RFDiffusion design as a sculptor / scriptwriter / critic relay, and the 200M-structure AlphaFold Database.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
