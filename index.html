Bioinformatics for Engineers

How biology became a data problem.

A one-semester tour of the instrumentation, signal processing, and algorithms that turned biology into an engineering discipline. From the physical structure of the genome to the algorithms that read it — sequencers as microscopes, basecallers as signal processors, the central dogma as a pipeline. Everything framed for engineers: where the signal is, where the noise is, and how the instrument was designed.

  16. 16 Student

    ML in Genomics: Architectures, Pitfalls, Frontiers

    A synthesis lecture pulling the architectural pattern out of L1–L15: pileups → CNN, long-range regulation → dilated convolution + transformer, count matrices → VAE, molecular graphs → GNN, protein 3D → equivariant transformer. The right likelihood beats a deeper stack, self-supervised pretraining is the workhorse for label-scarce domains, leakage-aware splits separate honest results from press releases — closing with an honest read of DNA language models (DNABERT, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo), cell foundation models (scGPT, Geneformer, scFoundation, UCE), plus an information-theory section linking inductive bias, motif IC, mutual-information coevolution, and the genetic code as an error-correcting code.

  17. 17 Student

    Clinical Genomics, Variant Interpretation, and Ethics

    When a pipeline's output informs a patient's diagnosis, dosing, or surgical decision, the rules change. CLIA / CAP / IVDR / FDA validation as regulated software, the ACMG/AMP rule table as an auditable Bayesian classifier, the variant evidence ecosystem (gnomAD, REVEL, AlphaMissense, SpliceAI, ClinVar), pharmacogenomics as discrete-state lookup (CYP2D6/codeine, VKORC1+CYP2C9/warfarin, HLA-B*57:01/abacavir, TPMT/thiopurines), an immunogenomics primer (HLA typing, V(D)J repertoire, neoantigen pipeline), incidental findings on the ACMG SF list, the 2024 FDA LDT rule, GINA's narrow protections, and ancestry bias as a training-distribution coverage problem.

    ≈ 4h 0min · 13 figures · 8 interactive tools
  18. 18 Student

    Cancer Genomics: Integrated Capstone

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  19. 19 Student

    BLAST and Sequence Search Statistics

    Heuristic search with statistical guarantees — the seed-and-extend cascade, BLOSUM/PAM substitution matrices, bit scores as log-odds, Karlin-Altschul E-values from extreme-value theory, PSI-BLAST iterative profile refinement, and modern fast alternatives (DIAMOND, MMseqs2, USEARCH) on the speed-vs-sensitivity Pareto frontier. The most-used scientific software in history, plus its replacements that trade modest sensitivity for 100-1000× speedup. Application patterns: genome annotation, ortholog identification (RBH), function transfer, horizontal-gene-transfer detection, drug-target off-target prediction.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  20. 20 Advanced

    Multiple Sequence Alignment, Phylogenetics, and Comparative Genomics

    From pairwise alignment to families. Progressive MSA decomposes the intractable exact-tensor DP into a sequence of pairwise steps along a guide tree. Phylogenetic trees are graphical models inferred by Felsenstein's pruning algorithm — the tree analog of forward-backward in HMMs. The molecular clock is a Poisson process; relaxed clocks + fossil calibration give absolute dates. dN/dS is a likelihood-ratio test for selection; comparative genomics aligns whole genomes via synteny. Closes with non-coding RNA secondary-structure inference (SCFGs, Infernal, Rfam) — the right model for nested base-pairing.

    ≈ 3h 55min · 13 figures · 8 interactive tools
  21. 21 Advanced

    HMMs, Profile HMMs, and Gene Finding

    State-space models on discrete observations. Forward (likelihood), Viterbi (decoding), Baum-Welch (EM training) — three algorithms, $O(T K^2)$ each. Profile HMMs operationalise MSAs into probabilistic family models; HMMER + Pfam annotate every new genome. GENSCAN and AUGUSTUS read a chromosome as a giant HMM tape; ChromHMM segments the epigenome; pair-HMMs power haplotype-aware variant callers. Where deep learning has replaced HMMs (regulatory prediction) and where HMMs still win (segmentation, small data, interpretable annotation).

    ≈ 3h 45min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  22. 22 Advanced

    Network Biology and Pathway Analysis

    From a gene list to biological meaning. Five network types — PPI, GRN, metabolic, signalling, disease — each with its own data sources and uses. Pathway enrichment via ORA (hypergeometric) and GSEA (Mann-Whitney / KS on the ranked list). Network propagation as a low-pass filter on the graph Laplacian; module detection as clustering in the graph spectrum. KEGG, Reactome, GO, and MSigDB compared head-to-head. Drug-target prediction with RWR; disease-gene prioritisation; hub-centrality bias. The whole field is graph signal processing applied to biology.

    ≈ 3h 15min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  23. 23 Advanced

    Metagenomics and the Microbiome

    A microbiome is a population assayed by sequencing. 16S amplicon sequencing is cheap and bacteria-only; shotgun metagenomics is expensive and multi-kingdom. DADA2 and QIIME2 turn 16S reads into ASVs, taxonomy, and diversity. Kraken2 + MetaPhlAn + HUMAnN turn shotgun reads into species abundances and pathway profiles. Diversity metrics generalise from population genetics. Compositional bias is everywhere; ANCOM-BC handles it. Gut microbiome predicts immunotherapy response; antibiotic-resistance genes track in real time across hospital ecosystems. The whole field is community signal demixing.

    ≈ 3h 30min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  24. 24 Advanced

    CRISPR Functional Screens and DepMap

    A genome-scale CRISPR screen is compressed sensing on a perturbation matrix. ~80,000 sgRNAs in lentivirus, MOI ~ 0.3, T0 vs T-final sequencing → which gene losses kill cells, which provide drug resistance. CRISPRko, CRISPRi, CRISPRa modalities each give distinct information. MAGeCK aggregates 4-6 sgRNAs per gene via rank-based statistics. DepMap turns this into a cancer-dependency atlas across ~1000 cell lines. MAVEs (saturation mutagenesis) systematically map every variant's effect — directly informing clinical interpretation. Most modern cancer drug targets emerge from this pipeline.

    ≈ 3h 20min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  25. 25 Advanced

    Causal Inference and Mendelian Randomisation

    Association is not causation. A GWAS hit means $P(D \mid X)$ shifts; it does not say what happens if you intervene on $X$. Mendelian randomisation uses germline SNPs as instrumental variables — Mendel's laws provide the randomisation an RCT can't. Three IV assumptions; three sensitivity tests (MR-Egger, MR-PRESSO, weighted median); one Steiger directionality check. Cis-MR de-risks drug development before clinical trials (PCSK9 inhibitors are the textbook case). The whole field is partial-derivative-under-intervention machinery applied to genomics.

    ≈ 3h 10min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  26. 26 Advanced

    Drug Discovery and Chemoinformatics

    Drug discovery as a 10–15-year, ~$2.6B funnel where attrition is the rule. Molecules become strings (SMILES, InChI), bit-vectors (Morgan/ECFP), and graphs (for GNNs). Tanimoto similarity, Lipinski Ro5, Veber, QED — drug-likeness as triage, not gospel. Vina docking to score poses; ADMET predictors to flag liabilities (hERG, AMES, DILI). Hit → lead → candidate runs DMTA cycles where AI shaves design time. AlphaFold opens orphan targets. Pharma screens 10⁵ from a 10⁶⁰ universe and approves ~5,000 drugs per century — every step in chemoinformatics is about making that ratio less terrible.

    ≈ 3h 25min · 12 figures · 7 interactive tools
  27. 27 Advanced

    Mass-Spectrometry Proteomics + Metabolomics

    RNA-seq counts transcripts; mass spec counts proteins (and metabolites). LC-MS/MS turns proteins into peptides, ionises them, fragments them along the backbone, and matches the b/y peaks to a database. Target-decoy gives FDR. DDA picks top-N; DIA scans windows (and reads as compressed sensing on a peptide library). LFQ vs SILAC vs TMT each trade off CV, multiplexing, and ratio compression. Then PTMs as codebook expansion, plasma biomarkers spanning 10 orders of magnitude, chemoproteomics finding what drugs actually bind, and metabolomics — same instrument, sparser reference, ~30–50% of peaks still unidentified.

    ≈ 3h 25min · 13 figures · 7 interactive tools
