Logo proof v2 — Variant A iterations

Three new directions, all riffs on the double-sine helix with rungs.

What's different from v1. The original Variant A used quadratic-Bézier curves that produced a soft, almost organic sine. These three variants sharpen different aspects: A1 tightens the geometry and adds a subtle directional sense; A2 is asymmetric — only the top strand undulates, giving it more of a "signal" reading; A3 keeps the double helix but makes the rungs the hero, colored with the base palette.
Variant A1

Tightened helix with lead-in

Same double-sine structure as original A, but the amplitude is tighter and the helix has a small lead-in on the left and right — thin straight extensions that make the mark read like a snippet of a longer signal. Rungs are evenly spaced. This version has the strongest "this is a fragment of a longer thing" character, which fits the course (a genome is a sequence, we're looking at a piece of it).

Variant A2

Asymmetric — one strand, one signal

Strips the double-helix symmetry: the bottom strand is a flat baseline, the top strand is a sine wave, and the rungs connect them as vertical bars of varying length. This reads much more as signal plotted against a reference — oscilloscope trace, sequencing current, Phred score bar chart. It's the most EE-coded of the three and the least literal as a helix. A calculated tradeoff.

Variant A3

Helix with base-colored rungs

Same geometry as the original Variant A, but the rungs themselves are the information: thicker, shorter, and colored with the A/T/G/C palette from the design system. The strands recede (thinner, `--fg-muted`), and the colored rungs become the visual center. This connects the mark directly to the color language used in every artifact across the site — whenever a student sees red/teal/amber/green bases in an artifact, they'll unconsciously connect it back to the logo.

Quick comparison

A1 — tightest helix, lead-in tails suggest "fragment of longer signal". Most refined version of the original direction. A2 — asymmetric, one flat strand + one sine. Reads as data plot / signal trace; weakest helix reading, strongest engineering reading. A3 — same helix geometry, rungs become colored A/T/G/C. Ties the mark directly to the site's base palette. The only version where the logo teaches something about itself.
